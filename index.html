Frequently Asked Questions ‚Äì Estate Planning & Probate in Staten Island

When should I start estate planning?

Estate planning is beneficial at any adult age, especially after acquiring property, starting a family, or building savings. Planning early helps protect your assets and ensures your wishes are legally documented.

What documents are included in a complete estate plan?

A comprehensive estate plan may include a will, trust, power of attorney, healthcare proxy, and beneficiary designations. The exact documents depend on your goals and personal circumstances.

How can estate planning help my family avoid probate delays?

Proper estate planning, including the use of trusts and clear beneficiary designations, can help reduce or avoid probate, allowing assets to be transferred more efficiently to your loved ones.

What happens if someone passes away without a will in New York?

If a person dies without a will, New York intestacy laws determine how assets are distributed. This process may not reflect the individual‚Äôs wishes and can lead to delays or family disputes.

Do estate plans need to be reviewed or updated over time?

Yes. Estate plans should be reviewed periodically and updated after major life changes such as marriage, divorce, relocation, new assets, or changes in beneficiaries.

Can your firm assist executors and administrators during probate?

Absolutely. We provide legal guidance to executors and administrators throughout the probate process, including court filings, asset distribution, and estate administration responsibilities.
